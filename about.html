Our Vision

We are driven by a fierce commitment to inspire breakthroughs in the way the world treats children and to achieve immediate and lasting change in their lives. Our goal is to ensure that every child attains the right to survival, protection, development, and participation. We work tirelessly across 19 states to reach the most marginalized children, providing them with the platform to not just survive, but to thrive in a safe and nurturing environment regardless of their background.

  • Defending rights during crises
  • Championing marginalized voices
  • Innovating for child survival
  • Building resilient communities

Our mission extends beyond simple aid; it is about systemic transformation that empowers children to become the architects of their own future. By partnering with the government, civil society, and the children themselves, we create a safety net that catches those who fall through the cracks. We are dedicated to ensuring that no child is ever left behind, forgotten, or silenced in the journey toward a better, more equitable India.

Our Vision

We envision a world in which every child attains the right to survival, protection, development, and participation. This vision guides every program we design and every partnership we forge, aiming for a society where children are respected, valued, and listened to. It is a future where the circumstances of a child’s birth do not dictate the outcome of their life, and where equity is the foundation of their growth, ensuring that opportunities are accessible to all.

  • Zero preventable child deaths
  • Quality education for all
  • Protection from all violence
  • Children leading the change

Our History

Our roots run deep in history, tracing back to 1931 when Mahatma Gandhi signed the Declaration of the Rights of the Child, drafted by our founder Eglantyne Jebb. This marked the first connection between the global movement and India's struggle for justice. In the early 1940s,

we began providing relief in India, supporting a child welfare centre in Kolkata and aiding families displaced by partition and famine, establishing a legacy of responding to humanitarian crises with speed and compassion.

Over the decades, we have evolved from a relief agency into a leading child rights organization that influences national policy and grassroots practice. From the streets of Mumbai to the flood plains of Assam, our history is written in the stories of the millions of children we have served.
